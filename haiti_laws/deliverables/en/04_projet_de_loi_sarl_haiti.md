# 4) Full draft law — Creation of the Limited Liability Company (SARL)

**Draft law creating the Limited Liability Company (SARL) and related provisions**

## Title I — General provisions

**Article 1 — Purpose**
This law establishes the Limited Liability Company (SARL), a commercial company with legal personality, intended for the conduct of economic activities and governed by the provisions below.

**Article 2 — Limited liability**
Partners’ liability is limited to the amount of their contributions. Partners are not liable on their personal assets for the company’s obligations, subject to provisions on management faults and expressly granted guarantees.

**Article 3 — Registered office and name**
The SARL is formed under a corporate name followed or preceded by the words “Société à responsabilité limitée” or the initials “SARL.” Its registered office is located in Haitian territory.

## Title II — Formation of the SARL

**Article 4 — Constitutive act**
The SARL is formed by written statutes. The statutes specify in particular:
1) the corporate name;
2) the corporate purpose;
3) the registered office;
4) the term;
5) the amount of share capital;
6) the identity of partners and the allocation of equity interests;
7) the designation of one or more managers and the scope of their powers.

**Article 5 — Partners**
The SARL may be formed by one or more persons. The maximum number of partners is determined by regulation.

**Article 6 — Registration**
The SARL acquires legal personality upon registration with the competent registry. Legal publication of the statutes and essential acts is organized by regulation.

## Title III — Capital and equity interests

**Article 7 — Share capital**
Share capital is set by the statutes. Contributions may be in cash or in kind. Contributions in kind are subject to an appraisal stated in the statutes.

**Article 8 — Equity interests**
Equity interests are registered. They grant each partner a right proportional to contributions, unless otherwise provided in the statutes in compliance with the law.

**Article 9 — Transfer of equity interests**
Transfers of equity interests to third parties are subject to partner approval under the conditions set out in the statutes. Transfers between partners are free, unless otherwise provided by the statutes.

**Article 10 — Capital changes**
Increases and reductions of capital are decided by the partners under the quorum and majority conditions set out in the statutes and this law. They are subject to the publication formalities provided in Article 6.

## Title IV — Governance and management

**Article 11 — Management**
The SARL is managed by one or more managers, whether partners or not, designated by the statutes or by decision of the partners.

**Article 12 — Manager’s powers**
The manager(s) are vested with the broadest powers to act in all circumstances on behalf of the company, within the limits of the corporate purpose and subject to powers expressly attributed to partners by law or the statutes.

**Article 13 — Liability of managers**
Managers are individually or jointly liable, as the case may be, for faults committed in their management in accordance with the general law of liability.

**Article 14 — Collective decisions**
Collective decisions of partners are taken under the quorum and majority conditions set out in the statutes. They are recorded in minutes kept at the registered office.

## Title V — Accounting and audit

**Article 15 — Accounting**
The SARL keeps regular accounts and retains its accounting documents in accordance with applicable legislation.

**Article 16 — Statutory audit**
A statutory auditor is appointed when the company exceeds thresholds for revenue, balance‑sheet total, or number of employees set by regulation.

**Article 17 — Allocation of results**
The partners’ meeting approves the accounts and decides on the allocation of results in accordance with the statutes and the law.

## Title VI — Transformation, dissolution, and liquidation

**Article 18 — Transformation**
The SARL may be transformed into another corporate form under the conditions provided by the statutes and the laws in force.

**Article 19 — Dissolution**
The company is dissolved upon expiry of its term, by decision of the partners, by completion or extinction of the corporate purpose, or by any other cause provided by law.

**Article 20 — Liquidation**
In the event of dissolution, liquidation is carried out in accordance with the rules of general law, subject to the specific provisions of this law.

## Title VII — Transitional and final provisions

**Article 21 — Transitional regime**
Existing companies may transform into SARLs under conditions set by regulation, without prejudice to creditors’ rights.

**Article 22 — Legislative coordination**
Provisions of the Commercial Code and Haitian commercial legislation contrary to this law are amended as needed to ensure coexistence of corporate forms.

**Article 23 — Entry into force**
This law enters into force on the date set by its publication in the Official Gazette.
