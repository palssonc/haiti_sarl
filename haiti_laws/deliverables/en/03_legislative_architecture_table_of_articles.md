# 3) Legislative architecture (Table of Articles) — Law creating the Haitian SARL

## General structure (civil‑law style)

### Title I — General provisions
- **Art. 1** — Purpose of the law and definition of the SARL
- **Art. 2** — Legal personality, limited liability, registered office
- **Art. 3** — Capacity and quality of partner

### Title II — Formation of the SARL
- **Art. 4** — Constitutive act (statutes) and mandatory mentions
- **Art. 5** — Number of partners, cash and in‑kind contributions
- **Art. 6** — Registration and legal publication

### Title III — Capital and equity interests
- **Art. 7** — Share capital, payment, and modalities
- **Art. 8** — Allocation of equity interests, joint ownership, usufruct
- **Art. 9** — Transfer and transmission of equity interests
- **Art. 10** — Capital increases and reductions

### Title IV — Governance and management
- **Art. 11** — Appointment of one or more managers
- **Art. 12** — Manager’s powers and statutory limits
- **Art. 13** — Civil and criminal liability of managers
- **Art. 14** — Collective decisions of partners

### Title V — Accounting and audit
- **Art. 15** — Accounting obligations and approval of accounts
- **Art. 16** — Statutory audit (thresholds and obligations)
- **Art. 17** — Allocation of results and reserves

### Title VI — Transformation, dissolution, and liquidation
- **Art. 18** — Transformation into another corporate form
- **Art. 19** — Grounds for dissolution
- **Art. 20** — Liquidation and closure

### Title VII — Transitional and final provisions
- **Art. 21** — Transitional regime for existing companies
- **Art. 22** — Coordination with the Commercial Code and related texts
- **Art. 23** — Entry into force

## Mapping “new” vs “amendments”

- **New law**: Titles I to VI (complete creation of the SARL).
- **Amendments**: Title VII (targeted modifications to the Commercial Code for compatibility and coexistence with SA/commandite).
