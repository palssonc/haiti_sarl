Unlock Haitian entrepreneurship with a proven legal tool.

A modern small-business limited-liability structure (SARL) is a simple fix used around the world—still missing in Haiti’s legal toolkit for SMEs.

Read the drafted law See how it works

The problem

Current options in Haiti create friction for founders and small firms.

Heavy entry requirements
The existing framework can be cumbersome for small ventures.
Uneven risk exposure
Some legal forms leave active partners with broader personal risk.
Mismatch for SMEs
Founders need limited liability with simpler governance from day one.
Lost formalization opportunities
Complex setup discourages businesses from entering the formal economy.

The solution: a Haitian SARL path

The drafted reform introduces an SME-friendly legal form with limited liability, clear rules, and practical governance.

Before

  • SMEs choose between structures that are often too rigid or too risky.
  • Formalization can feel legally complex for early-stage founders.
  • Governance rules are not optimized for small teams.

After (with SARL)

  • Limited-liability vehicle tailored to small and growing firms.
  • Clear article-based framework for incorporation and governance.
  • Better foundation for credit, investment, and compliance.

Implementation plan

  1. Months 1–3: Issue implementing regulations and publication templates.
  2. Months 4–6: Update registry workflows and train public/notarial actors.
  3. Months 6–12: Enable transition paths for existing firms.
  4. Months 3–12: Run SME outreach in French and Haitian Creole.

FAQ

Is this a final enacted law?

No. This site presents drafted legislative materials for review and advocacy.

Does this replace all existing company forms?

No. The proposal is designed to coexist with existing forms while filling the SME gap.

Is there already a French draft text?

Yes. The full drafted French text is available on the legislation page.
